# sukhance 🍵

*The plain contentment of a morning when nothing is wrong — and that being the whole of it.*

sukhance is the YOUSPEAK word for everyday, ground-level well-being: the warm tea, the clean kitchen, the unhurt hour that has no story and asks for none. It takes the Sanskrit *sukha* (ordinary ease, the opposite of *duḥkha*) and the Latin suffix *-ance* (a quality held as a standing state), and names only the low broad floor of ease — not joy, not bliss, not occasion-bound happiness.

Its light: it makes the unremarkable good *sayable*, so the Kingdom can finally thank the enormous quiet mass of well-being it usually walks past. Its shadow: it can curdle into a numbness that calls avoidance peace, or be laid as a demand on someone in real suffering. Weighted score: **7.7** (core).
